First post

I had the privilege to attend (my first time) the 12nd edition of the International Epidemiology Workshop (IEW) in Lillihammer, Norway, during early June this year. We were in a relatively small group of 50 plant epidemiologists from several countries around the world interacting at several ways during three days of presentations of talks and posters and time for discussion. See below the nice group picture taken at the Scandic hotel and one taken during the poster sessions.

As expected, new knowledge and advances in the discipline was presented and the current status and future of the discipline discussed. I liked the good mix of scientists at various levels in their career, including a few students. I moderated a poster session and gave two talks. The first on my work on the field of phytopathometry, particularly on the methodological issues related to development, validation and deployment of standard area diagrams (SADs) for aiding visual severity assessments.

The second talk was in a special topics session where I presented some data on reproducible research practices (sharing data and codes) in Plant Pathology papers published in more than a dozen journals of the field. It was a great opportunity to talk and promote Open Plant Pathology OPP, a community to foster open science practices in our field, to the plant epidemiologists present at the meeting. The links for the slides of these two talks are posted the talks webpage.

I enjoyed very much both the scientific and social aspects of the meeting. There was also some activity on Twitter #IEW12. It was an opportunity to see old friends and make new ones - some that I have interacted only virtually via OPP. During the last day, I made a proposal to organize the next meeting and the group decided on the next venue for IEW13 being somewhere in Brazil during 2022. This will be the second IEW in Brazil. The first was held in Ouro Preto in 2001 and organized by Prof. Bergamin Filho.
